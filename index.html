Nampa, Idaho

Location

Nampa is located in the Treasure Valley of southwestern Idaho, approxiamtely 20 miles west of Boise.

About

The city sits on a high-desert plain, bordered by farmlands and the foothills of the Rocky Mountains.

Population

Nampa is the third-largest city in Idaho with an estimated population of 117,350 people.

Economy

The economy in Nampa has historically been based on agriculture, but it has diversified to include manufacturing, food processing, and retail.

Community

Nampa is a community-oriented city and often hosts local farmers markets and festivals.

Weather

Nampa has a four-season, semi-arid climate with hot, dry summers and cold, snowy winters.

Sports

Some popular sports in Nampa would include the rodeo, softball, pickleball, and golf.

Things To Do

  • Explore the Deer Flat National Wildlife Refuge, a sanctuary for migratory birds and other wildlife with walking trails and fishing access.
  • Visit the Nampa Civic Center for live performances, concerts, and other entertainment events.
  • Stroll through downtown Nampa, where you can browse local shops, restuarants, and the Nampa Farmers market.
  • Spend a day at Lake Lowell, a reseivor offering fishing, boating, and swimming during the summer months.
